Egypt BasketBall Federation

  • IN 1920, The spread of the game of basketball began in Egypt, where some of the French established a club in Alexandria called the All Sports Club. It included basketball in its programs, and it is considered the first club that practiced basketball in Egypt.

  • IN 1922, The Young Men's Christian Association in Assiut introduced the game in the American way, as the members of the association were all Americans. Then the Young
    Men's Christian Association in Cairo followed suit and introduced it in 1925 AD, and the American University helped spread it in Cairo.

  • IN 1925, The Cairo Basketball District was established.

  • IN 1930, The Egyptian Basketball Federation was officially established from four regions: Cairo, Alexandria, Assiut and Port Said, and the number of teams was not more
    than ten, all of them foreign except for the Royal Guard team.

  • IN 1932, The Egyptian Basketball Federation joined the International Federation. The game developed and spread in schools, bodies, institutions, universities and clubs of
    foreign communities in Egypt, and all of these organized league competitions among themselves for men and women. The Royal Guard Basketball team won the Egyptian
    championship in the first competition organized by the Federation.

  • IN 1956, Joined the Arab Basketball Federation.

  • IN 1961, Joined the Organization of African Unions.

  • IN 1994, Construction of a five-a-side stadium
