Hand Ball

Handball is a team sport in which two teams of 7 players each compete (6 players and a goalkeeper) where the players pass the ball among themselves to try to throw it with their hands into the opponent’s goal to score a goal. 10 minutes, and the handball game in its current form was formed at the end of the nineteenth century in Northern Europe, and the first handball matches for men were held at the 1936 Summer Olympics in Berlin - Germany.The game of handball appeared for the first time in the Middle Ages, in France, on the island of Greenland among the Eskimo peoples, and in the continent of Africa, especially in the state of Egypt during the rule of the pharaohs. Handball appeared in the nineteenth century in many countries, such as Denmark, the Czech Republic, Ukraine, Slovakia, Germany, and Uruguay. At the end of the nineteenth century, the game of handball in its present form was formed in Northern Europe, especially in Germany, Denmark, Sweden and Norway.In 1926, the Legislative Council of the International Federation of Amateur Athletes assigned a special committee to draw up international rules that work on organizing special matches in the game of handball. In 1928, the International Handball Federation was formed. In 1946, the International Handball Federation was formed. In 1972, the game was entered into the Olympics, and it was adopted.

HandBall Rules

The team cannot maintain possession of the ball without trying to attack.

The start is awarded to the team that wins the toss.

The match begins with both teams in their respective halves.

A goal can be scored from any type of throw.

A player can hold the ball for a maximum of three seconds.

A player can dribble continuously, provided the ball bounces off the ground.

Players are unable to hit the opponent with the ball.
